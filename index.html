The honest starting point
Five problems we own and will solve directly.
Every structural decision in this plan maps back to one of these problems. Click each to expand.
Behavioral analytics is cobbled together — and the org is asking for more
Internal teams want to understand what's happening in the platform. Data is assembled from multiple inconsistent sources with low trust and low adoption. Despite strong demand, there is no reliable self-serve tool teams can log into and trust.
Critical
▾
Impact today
Product decisions made on intuition. Amplitude underutilized. Teams filing one-off analytics requests instead of self-serving. No single trusted metric for "time" — the org's most important measure.
How we solve it
Dedicated owner. Amplitude rebuilt to our specs with a UI the org will adopt. Single trusted behavioral data layer every PM can access independently. "Time" becomes a reliable, consistent number.
Data View lacks product foundation — and no one owns the Snowflake relationship
Data View is a real commercial product but lacks basic artifacts — documentation, tooling, usage visibility. No one is proactively managing the Snowflake relationship or evaluating new platform features for adoption. These two gaps are deeply connected.
Critical
▾
Impact today
Data View stagnates without clear product investment. Snowflake capabilities — including Cortex — evaluated reactively. The org is not getting full value from its most significant platform investment.
How we solve it
One owner for both Data View and Snowflake strategy. Product foundation built. Snowflake partnership managed proactively so new capabilities are adopted on our terms, not theirs.
Data teams are modeling and pushing to Snowflake with no guardrails and no shared platform
Analytics teams build models and pipelines independently — no standards, no governance, no common toolset. The result is inconsistent data quality, duplicated effort, and ongoing friction with the analytics organization.
High
▾
Impact today
AES is a friction source, not an enabler. No gold standard for data models. Engineering bottlenecked on requests that should be self-serve. Analytics org frustrated by inconsistency.
How we solve it
An internal platform where data teams model data and build pipelines the right way — automation, guardrails, governance baked in. Anyone in data builds gold-standard models without bottlenecking engineering.
We've built a strong reporting foundation — but custom analytics remains out of reach for smaller practices
Data View gives sophisticated customers direct access to their data, and our Sigma dashboard investment has meaningfully improved standard reporting. But smaller practices still can't get answers to the questions that matter most to them — the ones that don't fit a pre-built dashboard and don't justify the overhead of a full data feed. Custom analytics remains a gap for a significant portion of our customer base.
High
▾
Impact today
Smaller practices can't get custom analytics without significant overhead. Pre-built dashboards don't fit every workflow. The gap between what Data View offers and what standard dashboards provide leaves a large portion of the customer base underserved on custom reporting.
How we solve it
An AI-native reporting interface that builds on our existing foundation — any practice asks questions in natural language and gets charts and insights back. No data feed required. No pre-built dashboard required. Every client gets useful analytics, regardless of size or technical sophistication.
Data pipelines need tighter standards, observability, and speed
Growing pressure on pipeline architecture. Clients asking for data faster — even in near real-time. Observability gaps mean clients sometimes find problems before we do. Efficiency, governance, and speed all need focused attention.
Moderate
▾
Impact today
Pipeline issues surface client-side before internal detection. Real-time demands inconsistently met. No tiered classification distinguishing client-critical from internal pipelines.
How we solve it
Tiered pipeline observability. Governance standards. Near real-time capabilities evaluated and deployed where demand justifies the investment. Clients stop finding problems before we do.
Foundational reliability gaps we are also addressing
Known risks that have lacked dedicated product ownership. This structure gives them a home — with engineering partnership required to fully resolve.
Pipeline observability
No consistent monitoring framework distinguishing client-critical pipelines from internal ones. Issues surface to clients before internal teams detect them.
Product lead: Vijay / Lindsey Cohenno
Data lineage
No clear picture of where data came from, how it was transformed, or who touched it. Governance and trust are impossible without lineage.
Product lead: Suhas Vasu · AES governance workstream
Disaster recovery
Recovery expectations and SLAs for client-critical data products are not consistently defined or enforced across the platform.
Product lead: Suhas Vasu / Vijay Rajagopal

The organization
One zone. One mandate. Clear leadership across four areas.
This is a single, unified organization — not four separate teams with separate agendas. Leaders are expected to work across areas, share context, and hold each other accountable. Ownership defines accountability, not territory.
The one-zone principle
Every person in this org is expected to understand the full data value chain — not just their piece of it. As the organization matures, people will work across teams, share ownership of cross-cutting problems, and be evaluated on the health of the whole, not just their lane.

A Behavioral Analytics PM and an AI-Native Reporting PM. Both have clear mandates and immediate impact. These are the two highest-leverage hires in the org this year.
Decision 2

Bridget leads Client Analytics with a focused mandate — Sigma engineering, RBAC, legacy sunset, and one strong new hire. Alignment on expectations and the development path clarifies everything downstream.
Decision 3
Organizational alignment on the analytics working model
The boundary between our platform org and the Analytics & AI org needs to be explicit — swim lanes, shared OKRs, and a regular cross-team rhythm. Requires visible leadership alignment to stick.
The north star
What data as an advantage actually looks like.

A small practice in rural Ohio logs into athenahealth, asks how their revenue cycle performed last month compared to practices like theirs, and gets a clear answer in seconds — no account manager call, no data analyst, no pre-built dashboard.

An athenahealth PM sees whether a new feature is saving clinicians time — the most important metric — without waiting three weeks for an analytics request to come back.

A data team member builds a new gold-standard model in days, not months, because the self-serve platform is in place and bad data cannot reach production.

That is what this organization is being built to deliver.
